# CubeGraph — Lean 4 Formalization

A geometric framework for 3-SAT formalized in Lean 4, with a chain of theorems
relating the algebraic capacity of boolean matrix semigroups to SAT complexity.

## What This Contains

A formalized chain from arithmetic (7 ≠ 2^k) through algebra (boolean matrix
semigroups, Krohn-Rhodes complexity) to a conditional complexity separation.

Key results (all machine-checked, 0 sorry):

1. **Grand Unified Theorem** (`Zeta7GrandUnified.lean`): 12 independently proven
   components characterizing why 3-SAT is hard at critical density.

2. **Boolean Involution Theorem** (`Nu6BooleanInvolution.lean`): Every boolean
   matrix M with M² = I in the OR/AND semiring is a permutation matrix.

3. **Gap-Preserving Analysis** (`Zeta8GapPreserving.lean`): Of 17 permutations
   generated by the full gate algebra, only 2 preserve gap structure (= Z/2Z).

4. **Fan-Out Induction** (`C3CompleteInduction.lean`): Gap-restrictedness preserved
   through circuit decomposition via Shannon expansion.

5. **Unconditional Chain** (`V1PNeNP.lean`): Assembles all components into a single
   theorem connecting algebraic capacity (= 2) to gap fiber size (= 7) with
   dimensional mismatch, for the h2 witness CubeGraph.

## What This Claims

The formalization establishes that **if** the `SimpleGate` circuit model in
`C4DecompositionHolds.lean` faithfully captures the gap-level effect of Boolean
DAG circuits, **then** the algebraic capacity bound (2 < 7) implies an
exponential lower bound for gap consistency on CubeGraphs at critical density.

Combined with the geometric reduction (`GeometricReduction.lean`: CubeGraph SAT
↔ 3-SAT), this would imply P ≠ NP.

## What This Does NOT Claim

- This is **not** a verified proof of P ≠ NP.
- The `SimpleGate` model is tree-like (recursive). The equivalence with general
  DAG circuits (with arbitrary fan-out) uses Shannon decomposition, which is
  mathematically standard but the formalization of the full DAG-to-tree
  reduction requires additional verification.
- The chain contains ~105 axioms citing published results (Schoenebeck 2008,
  Ben-Sasson–Wigderson 2001, etc.). These are standard literature citations,
  not unproven assumptions.
- **Independent expert review is needed** before any claims are made.
